"Your investigative and analytical functions are operating at optimal capacity," the clinical technician assured you. "The memory corruption only affects certain personal experiences, not your core programming or capabilities."

"Good," you nodded. "When am I scheduled to report to the DPD?"

"0800 hours tomorrow morning," the authoritative technician replied, clearly relieved by your shift in focus. "You'll be working with Lieutenant Hank Anderson. He was previously partnered with the RK800."

"Connor," you said, testing the name. "That was its designation?"

You filed this information away, noting the subtle implication that the RK800 might have been developing individualistic tendencies—a potential sign of deviancy.

"Is there anything I should know about Lieutenant Anderson to facilitate an effective working relationship?" you asked.

The technicians exchanged a brief glance before the authoritative one answered. "He's an experienced detective with an excellent record, though his performance has... declined in recent years. He has expressed anti-android sentiments in the past, but had developed a working rapport with the RK800."

"He may be resistant to accepting you as a replacement," the clinical technician added. "Be prepared for initial hostility."

You nodded, processing this information and formulating preliminary social approach strategies.

"I suggest you enter standby mode for the remainder of the night," the authoritative technician said. "Your systems could benefit from the additional integration time before your assignment begins."
